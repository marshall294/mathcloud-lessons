Converting Fractions to Decimals and Percentages (and Vice Versa)

Introduction

Fractions, decimals, and percentages are different ways to represent parts of a whole. Being able to switch between these forms helps in many real-world situations such as shopping, statistics, and finance. In this lesson, we will define each form, explain conversion methods, and go through detailed examples.

Key Definitions

  • Fraction: A fraction represents part of a whole and is written as \( \frac{a}{b} \), where:
    • \( a \) is the numerator (the part).
    • \( b \) is the denominator (the total number of equal parts).
    Example: \( \frac{3}{4} \) means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.
  • Decimal: A decimal represents part of a whole using a decimal point. For example, 0.5 is equivalent to half of a whole.
  • Percentage: A percentage is a fraction expressed as a part of 100. For example, 25% means 25 parts out of 100, or \( \frac{25}{100} \).

Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

Example: Convert \( \frac{2}{5} \) to a decimal:

  1. Divide 2 by 5: \( 2 \div 5 = 0.4 \).
  2. Answer: \( \frac{2}{5} = 0.4 \).

Converting Fractions to Percentages

To convert a fraction to a percentage:

  1. Convert it to a decimal first by dividing the numerator by the denominator.
  2. Multiply the decimal by 100 to find the percentage.

Example: Convert \( \frac{3}{8} \) to a percentage:

  1. Divide 3 by 8: \( 3 \div 8 = 0.375 \).
  2. Multiply by 100: \( 0.375 \times 100 = 37.5\% \).
  3. Answer: \( \frac{3}{8} = 37.5\% \).

Converting Decimals to Fractions

Identify the place value of the last digit in the decimal. Write the decimal as a fraction over its place value and simplify.

Example: Convert 0.75 to a fraction:

  1. Since 0.75 has two decimal places, write it as \( \frac{75}{100} \).
  2. Simplify by dividing both the numerator and denominator by 25: \( \frac{75 \div 25}{100 \div 25} = \frac{3}{4} \).
  3. Answer: \( 0.75 = \frac{3}{4} \).

Converting Decimals to Percentages

Multiply the decimal by 100.

Example: Convert 0.56 to a percentage:

  1. Multiply \( 0.56 \times 100 = 56\% \).
  2. Answer: \( 0.56 = 56\% \).

Converting Percentages to Fractions

Write the percentage as a fraction over 100 and simplify.

Example: Convert 40% to a fraction:

  1. Write as \( \frac{40}{100} \).
  2. Simplify: \( \frac{40 \div 20}{100 \div 20} = \frac{2}{5} \).
  3. Answer: \( 40\% = \frac{2}{5} \).

Worked Examples (10 Detailed Problems)

Example 1: Convert 7/8 to a decimal and percentage:

  1. Divide 7 by 8: \(7 \div 8 = 0.875\).
  2. Multiply by 100: \(0.875 \times 100 = 87.5\%\).

Answer: \( \frac{7}{8} = 0.875 = 87.5\% \).

Example 2: Convert 5/6 to a decimal and percentage:

  1. Divide 5 by 6: \(5 \div 6 = 0.8333\) (repeating).
  2. Multiply by 100: \(0.8333 \times 100 = 83.33\%\).

Answer: \( \frac{5}{6} = 0.8333 = 83.33\% \).

Example 3: Convert 0.125 to a fraction and percentage:

  1. Write as \( \frac{125}{1000} \).
  2. Simplify: \( \frac{125 \div 125}{1000 \div 125} = \frac{1}{8} \).
  3. Multiply by 100: \(0.125 \times 100 = 12.5\%\).

Answer: \( 0.125 = \frac{1}{8} = 12.5\% \).

Example 4: Convert 0.9 to a fraction and percentage:

  1. Write as \( \frac{9}{10} \).
  2. Multiply by 100: \(0.9 \times 100 = 90\%\).

Answer: \( 0.9 = \frac{9}{10} = 90\% \).

Example 5: Convert 75% to a decimal and fraction:

  1. Divide by 100: \(75 \div 100 = 0.75\).
  2. Write as \( \frac{75}{100} \).
  3. Simplify: \( \frac{75 \div 25}{100 \div 25} = \frac{3}{4} \).

Answer: \( 75\% = 0.75 = \frac{3}{4} \).

Example 6: Convert 66.67% to a decimal and fraction:

  1. Divide by 100: \(66.67 \div 100 = 0.6667\).
  2. Approximate as \( \frac{2}{3} \).

Answer: \( 66.67\% = 0.6667 \approx \frac{2}{3} \).

Example 7: Convert 9/20 to a decimal and percentage:

  1. Divide 9 by 20: \(9 \div 20 = 0.45\).
  2. Multiply by 100: \(0.45 \times 100 = 45\%\).

Answer: \( \frac{9}{20} = 0.45 = 45\% \).

Example 8: Convert 11/25 to a decimal and percentage:

  1. Divide 11 by 25: \(11 \div 25 = 0.44\).
  2. Multiply by 100: \(0.44 \times 100 = 44\%\).

Answer: \( \frac{11}{25} = 0.44 = 44\% \).

Example 9: Convert 0.375 to a fraction and percentage:

  1. Write as \( \frac{375}{1000} \).
  2. Simplify: \( \frac{375 \div 125}{1000 \div 125} = \frac{3}{8} \).
  3. Multiply by 100: \(0.375 \times 100 = 37.5\%\).

Answer: \( 0.375 = \frac{3}{8} = 37.5\% \).

Example 10: Convert 20% to a decimal and fraction:

  1. Divide by 100: \(20 \div 100 = 0.2\).
  2. Write as \( \frac{20}{100} \).
  3. Simplify: \( \frac{20 \div 20}{100 \div 20} = \frac{1}{5} \).

Answer: \( 20\% = 0.2 = \frac{1}{5} \).

Conclusion

Mastering conversions between fractions, decimals, and percentages is essential for many aspects of math and everyday life. Practice these conversions regularly to improve fluency and confidence!
